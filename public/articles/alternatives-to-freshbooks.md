## Why do small agencies look for FreshBooks alternatives?

FreshBooks is a capable invoicing and accounting tool, but it has limitations that push agencies to look elsewhere as they grow.

The most common reasons agencies switch:

**Collections features are too basic.** FreshBooks sends automated reminders but they go out from FreshBooks, not from the agency owner's email. There is no way to log payment promises, record follow-up conversations, or track what was said to each client and when.

**Pricing increases quickly.** FreshBooks' base plan limits the number of billable clients. As client count grows, so does the monthly cost.

**No post-invoice workflow.** FreshBooks treats the invoice as the endpoint. For agencies with chronic late payers, there is no structured system for what happens next.

**Better accounting options exist.** For agencies that need full accounting software, QuickBooks and Xero offer more depth.

Understanding why FreshBooks is not working for you is the first step to picking the right alternative.

## Where does FreshBooks fall short?

### Definitions Block

**Post-invoice management**
The process of tracking, following up on, and collecting payment for invoices after they have been sent.

**Payment promise**
A client's stated commitment to pay by a specific date, documented during follow-up.

**Collections cadence**
A structured schedule of follow-up communications designed to recover outstanding invoice payments.

**Accounts receivable (AR)**
Money owed to a business by clients for work already completed.

**Automated reminder**
A payment follow-up sent automatically on a preset schedule, without manual action from the sender.

---

FreshBooks automated reminders are better than nothing, but they are generic, go out from a FreshBooks email address, and offer no customization beyond timing. Clients can tell they are automated, which makes them easy to ignore.

For agencies managing 20 or more clients with varying payment histories, FreshBooks provides no way to track which clients have promised to pay and when, which follow-ups have been sent, what was discussed in previous conversations, or how to escalate the tone over time.

The result is the agency owner ends up managing this in their head, in email threads, or in spreadsheets alongside the tool they are already paying for.

## The 8 best FreshBooks alternatives in 2026

### 1. Duely

Duely solves the specific problem that FreshBooks leaves unaddressed: what happens after the invoice is sent. While FreshBooks handles the accounting and invoicing layer, Duely handles the collections layer.

The key difference is that Duely sends payment reminders from the agency owner's own Gmail account, not from a billing system. To the client, the reminder looks like a personal email. This significantly increases response rates and preserves the working relationship in a way that branded billing system emails do not.

Duely also creates a full timeline per client showing every follow-up sent, every payment promise made, every partial payment received, and every note logged. The agency owner always knows exactly where each client stands without digging through email.

Key features:
* Send reminders from your own Gmail, not from Duely
* Track payment promises with dates and outcomes
* Log partial payments against outstanding balances
* Draft follow-ups in three tones: friendly, firm, or final notice
* Full chronological timeline per client
* QuickBooks, Xero, and Stripe integrations
* Automate reminder cadences at set frequency
* Client can mark invoice as paid from their end
* Export to CSV

**Best for:** Small agencies billing 10 to 100 clients who want structured, personal follow-up without the manual work

**Pricing:** $29/month. 7-day free trial, no credit card required.

### 2. QuickBooks

QuickBooks is the most comprehensive small business accounting software available and the most direct upgrade from FreshBooks if your primary need is financial management depth.

Where FreshBooks offers invoicing with light accounting, QuickBooks offers full accounting with invoicing. The difference matters when you need payroll, tax preparation, detailed profit and loss reporting, or multi-user access for a bookkeeper.

QuickBooks' collections features are similar to FreshBooks: automated reminders exist but are not specialized. Agencies that need both full accounting and a strong collections workflow often use QuickBooks for accounting and Duely for follow-ups, taking advantage of the direct integration between them.

Key features:
* Full accounting, bookkeeping, and payroll
* Invoice creation and automated reminders
* Expense tracking and bank reconciliation
* Detailed financial reporting
* Multi-user access
* Hundreds of third-party integrations

**Best for:** Agencies that outgrew FreshBooks and need full accounting software

**Pricing:** From $25/month

---

### 3. Xero

Xero is a strong alternative for agencies that want a cleaner, more modern interface than QuickBooks while still getting full accounting capabilities. It is particularly popular with agencies in the UK, Australia, and Canada.

Xero's invoicing features are comparable to FreshBooks and its automated reminders are more customizable. The real advantage over FreshBooks is in the accounting depth: bank feeds, reconciliation, multi-currency support, and a large ecosystem of integrations.

Like QuickBooks, Xero integrates directly with Duely for agencies that want to add a dedicated collections layer on top of their accounting software.

Key features:
* Clean, modern interface
* Invoice creation and customizable automated reminders
* Bank reconciliation and expense tracking
* Multi-currency support
* Strong mobile app
* Direct integration with Duely

**Best for:** Agencies wanting a more modern alternative to QuickBooks with strong accounting features

**Pricing:** From $13/month

---

### 4. Wave

Wave is the natural FreshBooks alternative for agencies that want to reduce software costs without losing core invoicing and accounting functionality. Its free plan covers invoicing, expense tracking, and basic reporting.

The tradeoff is in depth and support. Wave does not offer payroll in all regions, its automated reminders are basic, and customer support on the free plan is limited. For agencies that need more advanced collections features, Wave has the same gap as FreshBooks: the tool stops at sending the invoice.

Key features:
* Free invoicing and accounting
* Expense tracking and basic reporting
* Automated payment reminders
* Payment processing available (transaction fees apply)
* Clean interface for basic needs

**Best for:** Early-stage agencies switching from FreshBooks to reduce costs

**Pricing:** Free plan available. Transaction fees apply for payment processing.

---

### 5. Zoho Invoice

Zoho Invoice is a strong FreshBooks alternative for agencies already using other Zoho products. It integrates natively with Zoho CRM, Zoho Books, and the broader Zoho suite, creating a connected workflow across sales, accounting, and invoicing.

As a standalone tool, Zoho Invoice is competitive with FreshBooks on features and cheaper at the entry level. Its automated reminders go out from Zoho rather than the user's email, which limits personalization. There is no dedicated collections workflow for tracking promises or follow-up history.

Key features:
* Invoice creation and automated reminders
* Time tracking and expense management
* Client portal
* Zoho suite integration
* Free for up to 5 clients

**Best for:** Agencies embedded in the Zoho ecosystem looking to replace FreshBooks without adding a new vendor

**Pricing:** Free for up to 5 clients. Paid plans from $15/month.

---

### 6. Bill.com

Bill.com is a step up in complexity and capability from FreshBooks, designed for agencies processing higher invoice volumes with more complex approval workflows. It automates both accounts payable and accounts receivable, making it useful for agencies that manage significant vendor payments alongside client invoicing.

For a small agency owner personally managing 10 to 30 client relationships, Bill.com is likely more complex and expensive than needed. Its strength is in processing volume and workflow automation at scale, not in the personalized follow-up communication that small agency collections requires.

Key features:
* Accounts payable and receivable automation
* Approval workflows and audit trails
* Automated payment reminders
* ACH and check payment processing
* QuickBooks and Xero integration

**Best for:** Agencies with higher invoice volumes or complex multi-approver payment workflows

**Pricing:** From $39/month

---

### 7. Invoice Ninja

Invoice Ninja is a free and open-source FreshBooks alternative with a strong feature set for freelancers and small agencies that want to avoid monthly software fees. It covers invoice creation, recurring billing, time tracking, and automated reminders across multiple payment gateways.

The open-source model makes it highly customizable for technical users. For agency owners without a technical background, the setup complexity and lack of dedicated support can be a barrier. Collections features are basic and there is no structured follow-up workflow.

Key features:
* Free and open-source
* Invoice creation and recurring billing
* Time tracking
* Multiple payment gateway integrations
* Self-hosting option for full data control

**Best for:** Technical agency owners who want a free, flexible alternative to FreshBooks

**Pricing:** Free plan available. Paid plans from $10/month.

---

### 8. HoneyBook

HoneyBook is a client management platform designed specifically for creative freelancers and small agencies. It combines proposals, contracts, invoicing, and payment collection in a single workflow, which makes it useful for agencies that want to manage the entire client lifecycle in one tool.

Its invoicing and payment features are strong for the creative services market. Collections features are limited to automated reminders and there is no dedicated follow-up tracking system. HoneyBook is better suited to agencies that want a full client management experience than those specifically looking to improve their collections process.

Key features:
* Proposals, contracts, and invoicing in one workflow
* Automated payment reminders and schedules
* Client portal and communication tools
* Project management features
* Integrates with QuickBooks

**Best for:** Creative agencies wanting to manage the full client lifecycle from proposal to payment

**Pricing:** From $16/month

## Which FreshBooks alternative is right for your agency?

**If FreshBooks is not helping you chase unpaid invoices:** Duely fills exactly that gap. It is not a FreshBooks replacement for accounting, it is what FreshBooks cannot do after the invoice is sent.

**If you need more accounting depth:** QuickBooks if you want the most comprehensive platform, Xero if you prefer a cleaner interface.

**If you want to reduce software costs:** Wave handles the basics for free. Invoice Ninja is the option for technical users who want more control.

**If you manage the full client lifecycle:** HoneyBook covers proposals through payment in one workflow.

Many small agencies end up using two tools: an accounting platform for the books and Duely for collections. If you already use FreshBooks for accounting and are only switching because of the collections gap, adding Duely rather than switching your accounting software entirely is often the simpler move.

## FAQ

### What is the biggest weakness of FreshBooks for small agencies?

FreshBooks has no dedicated post-invoice collections workflow. Automated reminders go out from FreshBooks rather than from the agency owner's email, and there is no way to track payment promises, log follow-up conversations, or manage partial payments. For agencies with chronic late payers, this gap becomes a real operational problem.

### Is Duely a replacement for FreshBooks?

No. Duely does not handle accounting, invoicing creation, or expense tracking. It picks up where FreshBooks stops, handling the follow-up, collections, and communication tracking after the invoice is sent. Duely and FreshBooks can be used alongside each other.

### What is the cheapest FreshBooks alternative?

Wave is free for core invoicing and accounting. Invoice Ninja has a free plan with more customization. Zoho Invoice is free for up to 5 clients. None of these include dedicated collections features, but they cover basic invoicing at no cost.

### Can I use Duely if I stay on FreshBooks?

Yes. Duely integrates with QuickBooks, Xero, and Stripe. If you use FreshBooks for invoicing and accounting, you can manage your follow-up workflow in Duely separately. The integration is not direct but the tools serve different functions and can work alongside each other.

### Which FreshBooks alternative is best for agencies in the UK and Australia?

Xero has particularly strong adoption in the UK and Australia and offers multi-currency support. Duely is designed for agencies in the US, UK, Canada, and Australia and handles follow-ups regardless of geography.
