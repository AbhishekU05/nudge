## Why freelancers look for Wave alternatives

While Wave has established itself as a well-known tool in the freelance and small agency ecosystem, many business owners eventually hit a wall with its capabilities. Wave is often praised for its initial setup—helping you get your business off the ground, organize your preliminary client roster, and send out those crucial first invoices. It provides a sense of professionalism that is essential when you are just starting to build your brand and need to present a cohesive image to your clients. The ability to quickly generate an invoice from a template, perhaps attach a rudimentary contract, and hit send gives many new freelancers a false sense of security. They assume that because the invoice was sent through a professional platform, the payment will naturally follow in a timely and orderly fashion.

However, the reality of running a freelance business or a small agency is rarely so straightforward. The true test of your business operations doesn't happen when the invoice is sent; it happens in the days, weeks, and sometimes months after the due date has passed. This is the critical post-invoice gap where Wave often falls drastically short. When an invoice becomes overdue, Wave typically offers only basic, rigid automated reminders. It lacks a dedicated, nuanced workflow for post-invoice collections. You can't easily track granular payment promises, manage complex partial payments without creating messy new invoices, or send intelligently escalated follow-ups based on specific client contexts. For freelancers who struggle with late-paying clients—which is nearly everyone in the B2B space—this gap means falling back on messy spreadsheets, manual calendar reminders, and scattered email threads to chase down what they're owed. This manual chaotic process defeats the entire purpose of investing in an all-in-one business management tool.


## The 5 best Wave alternatives

### Duely
**Best for:** Comprehensive post-invoice collections and automated follow-ups.

**What it does well:** 
The only tool focused exclusively on what happens after you send the invoice — follow-up tracking, payment promise logging, partial payment management, and Gmail-connected automated reminders. Duely seamlessly connects with your existing accounting tools like QuickBooks or Xero, automatically importing outstanding invoices so you don't have to duplicate data entry. It provides a centralized, timeline-based dashboard where you can log every single client interaction regarding a payment. Whether it's a promised payment date discussed over WhatsApp, a partial payment received via direct bank transfer, or a voicemail left on a Tuesday afternoon, Duely ensures you never lose track of a follow-up. By connecting directly to your Gmail, it sends automated, escalating reminders that actually look like they came from you personally, drastically increasing response rates compared to generic system-generated emails. It is the ultimate safeguard against revenue leakage for service-based businesses.

**What it doesn't do:** 
Duely is purposely not a full CRM, project management tool, or initial invoice generator. It does not handle top-of-funnel lead generation, complex contract e-signatures, or the creation of the original invoice document. This is a deliberate design choice, meaning you will need to use it alongside your existing invoicing or accounting software, focusing entirely on solving the collections gap rather than trying to do everything adequately.

**Price:** $29/month.


### FreshBooks
**Best for:** Small businesses and growing agencies needing robust, traditional double-entry accounting features.

**What it does well:** 
FreshBooks shines brightly in its core accounting capabilities. It offers strict double-entry accounting, comprehensive expense tracking with receipt scanning, detailed financial reporting, and excellent time-tracking integrations. It's incredibly user-friendly for non-accountants while still providing the depth required by growing agencies, partnerships, and freelancers who have scaled beyond simple income/expense tracking and need proper financial statements for tax time or securing business loans.

**What it doesn't do:** 
FreshBooks is primarily an accounting tool, not a dedicated collections CRM. While it allows you to send late payment reminders, it does not offer a detailed timeline view of follow-up communications per client or the ability to log specific payment promises made outside of the platform (like over a phone call or text message). If a client is perpetually late, FreshBooks won't help you strategize the follow-up process or address the human element of collections.

**Price:** Starts at $19/month for the Lite plan.


### Zoho Invoice
**Best for:** Small businesses needing completely free, highly customizable invoicing software.

**What it does well:** 
Zoho Invoice recently made their platform completely free for small businesses, which is a massive value add. It offers incredibly customizable invoice templates, excellent multi-currency support, a robust customer portal, and seamless integration with the massive ecosystem of other Zoho products. It is surprisingly feature-rich for a free product, allowing for automated workflows, estimates, and detailed tax handling.

**What it doesn't do:** 
Despite its deep invoicing features, Zoho Invoice is not a collections management tool. It does not provide a specialized interface for tracking the nuances of late payments, logging granular payment promises from clients, or managing a strategic, multi-channel follow-up process. It will tell you an invoice is late, but it won't give you a dedicated workspace to manage the process of getting it paid, leaving you to manage collections manually.

**Price:** Completely free.


### QuickBooks
**Best for:** Established businesses and agencies that need industry-standard accounting software supported by every CPA.

**What it does well:** 
QuickBooks is the undisputed industry standard for small business accounting. It offers unparalleled depth in reporting, inventory management, payroll integration, and tax preparation. Because it is ubiquitous, virtually every accountant and bookkeeper knows how to use it, making it incredibly easy to hand off your financials to a professional at the end of the year. It integrates with almost every other business tool on the market.

**What it doesn't do:** 
QuickBooks is notoriously complex and can be massive overkill for a solo freelancer. More importantly, its interface for dealing with outstanding invoices is purely transactional. It lacks any CRM-like features for managing the human element of collections—you cannot log conversational notes about why a payment is late, track specific future payment promises, or set up nuanced, personalized reminder sequences that adapt to the client's behavior, leading to a significant collections management gap.

**Price:** Starts at $30/month for Simple Start.


### Bonsai
**Best for:** Freelancers wanting a beautifully designed, all-in-one business management suite.

**What it does well:** 
Bonsai offers a robust, highly polished suite of tools tailored specifically for independent workers and creative freelancers. It covers almost the entire lifecycle of a client project, including beautifully crafted proposals, legally vetted contract templates, integrated time tracking, task management, and standard invoicing. It provides a very professional client portal that makes your business look highly established from the very first touchpoint. The seamless flow from a signed proposal directly into an active project and an initial invoice is one of its strongest selling points, saving significant administrative time during client onboarding.

**What it doesn't do:** 
While Bonsai handles the initial invoicing exceptionally well, its post-invoice collections workflow is limited to basic, rigid automated reminders. It lacks advanced, dedicated CRM features for tracking nuanced payment promises or managing complex partial payment scenarios where a client pays a fraction of the invoice directly to your bank account over irregular intervals. You cannot easily log manual phone calls or specific communication notes related strictly to accounts receivable, leaving a significant post-invoice gap in an otherwise comprehensive suite.

**Price:** Starts at $25/month for the Starter plan.


## How to choose

When deciding on the right Wave alternative for your freelance business or agency, the first and most crucial step is to brutally analyze the core bottleneck in your current operations. If your primary struggle is organizing the pre-sale process—creating beautiful, persuasive proposals, getting complex contracts signed efficiently, and managing top-of-funnel leads—then a lateral move to another all-in-one CRM might be the most appropriate choice. Platforms that excel at the front-end of the client relationship ensure you look highly professional, organized, and responsive from the very first interaction, which can help you win more business.

However, if your pre-sale workflow is already functioning relatively well, or if you prefer using specialized, individual tools (like sending a simple PDF proposal and a dedicated e-signature link via a different platform), your real operational challenge might lie entirely in the post-sale phase. Many freelancers find that generating and sending an invoice is the easy part; actually collecting the payment on time is a persistent, stressful, and time-consuming challenge. If you find yourself constantly checking your bank account with anxiety, drafting awkward follow-up emails late at night, or losing track of which client promised to pay when, an all-in-one tool is likely massive overkill and won't actually solve your primary, most painful problem. In fact, migrating to a new all-in-one tool might introduce new complexities without fixing the cash flow issues.

In this scenario, pairing your existing, comfortable invoicing tool with a dedicated collections management platform is by far the most effective strategy. Instead of ripping and replacing your entire tech stack for a new CRM that still falls short on the collections front, you can strategically bolt on a specialized tool designed specifically to handle the post-invoice reality. This best-of-breed approach allows you to maintain the accounting and invoicing workflow you are already comfortable with, while gaining professional, automated, and highly trackable collections capabilities. It separates the transactional act of billing from the relationship-driven act of collecting, giving you the best of both worlds.
