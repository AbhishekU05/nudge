# Duely vs Chaser: Which Invoice Collections Tool Is Right for Your Agency?

If you run a small marketing, design, web, or content agency and you're looking for a way to stop chasing invoices manually, you've probably come across both Duely and Chaser. They solve a similar problem but they're built for very different businesses. This page breaks down the difference so you can make the right call.

---

## The short version

Chaser is a powerful accounts receivable platform built for finance teams at mid-size and enterprise businesses. It has deep automation, credit monitoring, SMS reminders, auto-calls, and a dedicated collections service you can bolt on.

Duely is built specifically for small agency owners who are personally chasing invoices. It handles everything after you send an invoice: automated Gmail reminders sent from your own account, payment promise tracking, partial payment logging, follow-up drafting in multiple tones, and a full client timeline. It connects to QuickBooks, Xero, and Stripe.

If you're a 5-person agency owner who needs to stop manually tracking who owes what and why: Duely.

If you're a finance director at a company doing $10M+ in revenue who needs enterprise-grade AR automation with credit scoring and outsourced collections: Chaser.

## Who each product is built for

**Chaser** targets mid-size businesses and enterprise finance teams. Their feature set reflects this: credit monitoring, late payment predictors, SMS reminders, auto-call functionality, paper letters, in-app phone calls, revenue forecasting, receivables forecasting, cash flow forecasting, and an optional outsourced AR specialist service. These are powerful tools for a finance team managing hundreds of accounts across multiple entities.

**Duely** targets owner-led agencies with 1-20 employees billing 10-100 clients. The owner is the one chasing invoices, not a finance department. Duely is built around that reality: reminders go out from your own Gmail so they never feel like automated spam, follow-ups are drafted in the tone you want (friendly, firm, or final notice), and every client has a full timeline of promises, payments, and notes so you always know where things stand before you reach out.

## The key difference: whose name is on the reminder

Chaser sends reminders from Chaser's system. Your client receives an automated email from a collections platform.

Duely sends reminders from your own Gmail account. Your client receives an email that looks exactly like one you wrote yourself. No Duely branding. No indication it was automated.

For small agencies where the client relationship is everything, this matters. An automated reminder from a third-party collections tool can feel cold and transactional. A reminder that appears to come directly from you preserves the relationship while still doing the work automatically.

## When Chaser makes sense

- Your business has a finance team, not just a founder
- You're managing hundreds of invoices across multiple entities
- You need credit scoring on new clients before you take them on
- You want to outsource the actual collections work to a specialist
- You're doing more than $5M in annual revenue and can justify enterprise-level AR tooling

## When Duely makes sense

- You're the owner and you're personally chasing invoices
- You run a marketing, design, web, or content agency with 1-20 people
- You bill 10-100 clients on project or retainer terms
- You want reminders that come from your own Gmail, not a collections platform
- You need to track what clients have promised and what's actually been paid
- You're paying $259/month for Chaser and not using half the features
